Frequently Asked Questions

Why isn't the song listed in the YouTube description?

Many creators use royalty-free music from libraries like Epidemic Sound, Artlist, or AudioJungle but don't always credit specific tracks. Others use copyrighted music and don't list it. Our tool identifies the actual song regardless of what's in the description.

Can it find music from YouTube Shorts?

Yes! YouTube Shorts work exactly like regular YouTube videos. Just copy the Shorts URL and paste it into our tool. We'll identify the music the same way.

What if the YouTuber is talking over the music?

This is extremely common - vlogs, commentary videos, tutorials, and reviews all feature dialogue over background music. Our audio filtering technology isolates the background music track even when there's constant speech or narration.

Does it work with unlisted YouTube videos?

Yes, as long as you have the URL and can access the video yourself, our tool can analyze it. Unlisted videos, age-restricted videos (if you're signed in), and region-restricted videos (if accessible to you) all work.

Can I find songs from old YouTube videos?

Absolutely! Our tool works with YouTube videos from any time period - whether it's a video from 2005 or uploaded today. The audio quality might affect accuracy, but we can identify most music regardless of video age.

What about YouTube content with multiple songs?

Many videos use different songs for intro, main content, and outro. Our tool samples multiple segments automatically, so we'll typically identify the most prominent song or the one playing at the beginning of the video.
