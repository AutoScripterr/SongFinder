Method 1: From Video Links (Fastest)

Best for: Songs from TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, or any video

Steps:

  1. Copy the video URL
  2. Paste into our song finder
  3. Get instant results

Success rate: 95%+ for videos with clear audio

Method 2: By Humming or Singing

Best for: When you remember the melody but not the lyrics

Tools:

  • Google - Open Google app, tap mic, select "Search a song"
  • SoundHound - Hum or sing into the app

Success rate: 75-85% depending on how well you hum

Method 3: By Lyrics

Best for: When you remember some words from the song

Steps:

  1. Type lyrics in Google with quotation marks: "lyrics you remember"
  2. Google shows the song instantly

Alternative: Use Genius.com or Musixmatch lyric search

Success rate: 90%+ if lyrics are correct

Method 4: With Live Audio

Best for: Songs playing right now in stores, radio, or around you

Tools:

  • Shazam - Open app and tap the button
  • Google Assistant - Say "What song is this?"
  • Siri - Ask "What song is playing?"

Success rate: 90%+ in quiet environments

Method 5: By Description

Best for: When you remember details about the song

Where to ask:

  • r/NameThatSong on Reddit
  • r/TipOfMyTongue on Reddit
  • Music identification forums

Tip: Describe genre, decade, male/female voice, mood, etc.

Pro Tips for Finding Songs

  • Try multiple methods - If one doesn't work, try another
  • Check video comments - Someone may have already asked
  • Look for timestamps - In YouTube videos, check description for music timestamps
  • Use quotation marks - When searching lyrics on Google
  • Be patient - Obscure songs may need community help

Common Problems and Solutions

"The song is in another language"

Use our tool if you have a video. It works regardless of language.

"It's an old/obscure song"

Try r/NameThatSong on Reddit. The community is excellent at finding rare songs.

"It's background music from a video"

Perfect for our tool. We extract and identify background music.

"I keep mishearing the lyrics"

Try humming the melody with Google or SoundHound instead.
